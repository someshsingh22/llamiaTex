\subsection{Headline thesis the experiments must defend}
\label{sec:todo_narrative}

The paper claims \emph{one} thesis:
\textbf{verbalized agent access (text-mediated tool calling, even when its
invocation policy is RL-optimized) is structurally insufficient for
\emph{sustained} LLM--agent collaboration; latent-state internalization with
dynamic re-encoding closes that gap}.
Every experiment, ablation, and figure must be auditable against
exactly one of:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Verbalization is the bottleneck}
    (deltas vs.\ text-mediated baselines on tasks that require sustained
    interaction; cf.\ \cite{toolrl2025} as the strongest verbalized-RL
    baseline).
    \item \textbf{Dynamic re-encoding is the load-bearing component}
    (asymmetric degradation: multi-step vs.\ single-step;
    Sec.~\ref{sec:invoke_ablation}).
    \item \textbf{It generalizes beyond chess}
    (Sec.~\ref{sec:todo_extensions}; reviewers LEvg/HUaR repeatedly flagged
    chess-only framing; the rejected \texttt{Maia4All} review shows this
    omission can sink a paper on its own).
    \item \textbf{Mechanistic plausibility} (latent tokens encode
    interpretable, instruction-controllable structure;
    Sec.~\ref{sec:todo_sae}).
    \item \textbf{Scaling story} (joint scaling of LLM-side and
    agent-side intelligence; Sec.~\ref{sec:todo_scaling}).
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{External thesis-supporting citation we should lean on hard.}
\cite{yRxX01oRIi-cot-hurts} (NeurIPS'25 poster; ``Why CoT Sometimes Hurts'')
formalizes a 3-stage CoT noise decomposition $(\alpha_k, \epsilon_k)$ for
decomposition / solving / summarization, and shows empirically that
\emph{more reasoning tokens compound errors on inductive tasks}.
This is the cleanest external argument that verbalization is structurally
worse than latent conditioning, and it should appear in
\S\ref{sec:methodology} as the formal motivation for re-encoding rather than
re-verbalization.

\subsection{Sustained-interaction stress tests}
\label{sec:todo_sustained}

The thesis hinges on \emph{sustained} collaboration, but most current metrics
are single-shot. Add the following.

\textbf{[P0] Game-level commentary -- replay graph.}
Per game, plot timestep-aligned trajectories of
(i) engine evaluation, (ii) commentary attention to the played move,
(iii) per-move annotation accuracy. Define ``narrative continuity'' as
correlation between commentary salience and second derivative of engine eval
(turning points). LLAMIA should track turning points; tool-calling should
miss them.

\textbf{[P0] ICP turn-by-turn budget curve.}
Per ICP game log per move:
(a) win-rate evolution (Monte Carlo with $\geq 100$ rollouts via Lc0),
(b) deviation from base-policy KL,
(c) whether the move matched the instructed strategic theme (a held-out
LLM-judge classifier validated against $\geq 100$ human-labeled examples,
$\kappa \geq 0.7$ -- cf.\ \cite{yRxX01oRIi-cot-hurts}).
Plot expected win-rate vs.\ move number. Tool-calling should peak early;
LLAMIA's gap should widen with depth -- the most direct evidence that
re-encoding compounds.

\textbf{[P0] Multi-step commentary fidelity vs.\ horizon.}
Truncate commentary at first $N$ moves; ask raters to predict game outcome
(centipawn at end). Plot accuracy vs.\ $N$. Tool-calling plateaus;
LLAMIA continues to improve. Mirrors Allie \cite{Allie-bc2H72hGxB}'s
phase-of-game decomposition request.

\textbf{[P1] $k$-step rollout BC accuracy.}
Single-step BC accuracy vs.\ rollout BC accuracy at $k=1,3,5,8$.
Verbalization penalty should compound; LLAMIA's slope should be flatter than
text-mediated baselines.

\textbf{[P1] Phase-of-game decomposition.}
Opening / middlegame / endgame breakdown of every gameplay-related metric
(Elo, BC accuracy, commentary G-eval, ICP win-rate). \cite{Allie-bc2H72hGxB}
reviewers demanded this in rebuttal; treating it as a default makes our
results more legible.

\subsection{Mechanistic interpretability via Sparse Autoencoders}
\label{sec:todo_sae}

The call log nominated this as a flagship analysis. The two
\emph{rejected} Maia-2 SAE papers (\cite{gBz9NMbvYS-maia2-sae,Wxl0JMgDoU-maia2-sae})
provide a clear lesson: feature visualization alone is insufficient; reviewers
demand causal interventions, comparisons against alternative interpretability
methods, and human-grounded ecological validity.

\textbf{[P0] Convergent multi-method evidence.}
Following \cite{Leela-lookahead-8zg9sO4ttV}'s template, support every claim
about the $K{=}16$ latent tokens with three independent lines of evidence:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \emph{Sparse autoencoder.} Top-$k$ or JumpReLU SAE on LLM-side
    embeddings of the projected tokens. Dictionary $\in\{8\text{k},32\text{k}\}$,
    $L_0 \approx 32$, ghost-grad. Hyperparameter selection criterion follows
    \cite{gBz9NMbvYS-maia2-sae}: jointly optimize reconstruction and
    \emph{Concept-Coverage AUC} (CC-AUC) against a curated set of chess
    concepts (piece type, square color, pawn structure, king safety,
    mate-in-$n$, endgame type, opening family, tactical motifs).
    \item \emph{Linear probes.} Train probes for each concept directly on
    $\vz_s$ and $\vh_s$. Reviewers in
    \cite{gBz9NMbvYS-maia2-sae,Wxl0JMgDoU-maia2-sae} explicitly
    demanded this comparison; \cite{BLOIB8CwBI-soundness} shows probes can be
    decodable but causally inert. Report probe accuracy and intervention
    accuracy per concept.
    \item \emph{Activation patching.} Pair (clean FEN, corrupted FEN) such
    that Lc0 changes top move but a weak engine does not. Patch latent
    tokens between conditions; measure log-odds change on next-move
    prediction. Direct adaptation of \cite{Leela-lookahead-8zg9sO4ttV}'s
    protocol.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{[P0] $\pi$ vs.\ $\pi'$ feature-delta experiment (call log flagship).}
For one fixed position $s$, harvest:
$\vz_s^{\text{base}}$ (neutral instruction),
$\vz_s^{\text{ICP}}$ (e.g.\ ``opponent weak in endgames''),
$\vz_s^{\text{Maia}}$ (e.g.\ ``play like 1500 Elo'').
Decompose via SAE; show sparse delta $\Delta f$.
Hypothesis: ICP $\Delta f$ concentrates on endgame features;
Maia $\Delta f$ matches the per-feature delta of \emph{actual Maia-1500}
relative to base Lc0 (Maia is public; we have ground truth).
Publishable headline: \emph{instruction reproduces what fine-tuning does to a
separate model}.

\textbf{[P0] Key figure: 1 position $\times$ 3 instructions $\times$ 3 SAE views.}
Layout: 3 rows (instructions) $\times$ 3 columns (board with arrows, top-K
activated SAE features, board heatmap of feature attribution). One ICP
example, one BC example. Call log explicitly nominated this as the
``one example has to be the key figure'' figure.

\textbf{[P0] Causal steering experiment.}
Identify the SAE direction most predictive of an ICP instruction.
\emph{Ablate} the instruction prompt but \emph{add} the SAE direction at
inference time. Recovery of the win-rate delta is causal evidence.
This is the missing piece in \cite{gBz9NMbvYS-maia2-sae,Wxl0JMgDoU-maia2-sae}
that contributed to their rejection.

\textbf{[P0] Future-move-square probe (implicit lookahead).}
Linear probes on $\vz_s$ predicting the principal-variation squares 1, 3, 5
moves in the future. Direct adaptation of
\cite{Leela-lookahead-8zg9sO4ttV} \S 3.3. Report probe accuracy on solved
mate-in-$N$ puzzles and on quiet positions; the gap argues that LLAMIA
inherits implicit search.

\textbf{[P0] Per-token ablation (which of the 16 tokens does what?).}
Ablate one of $K{=}16$ tokens at a time; plot log-odds reduction across tasks.
Group tokens by ablation effect (gameplay-critical, commentary-critical,
ICP-critical). Mirrors \cite{Leela-lookahead-8zg9sO4ttV}'s per-(layer, head)
heatmap. Ties directly to the existing $k$-sweep ablation in
\S\ref{sec:token_ablation}.

\textbf{[P0] First-$N$ (not cherry-picked) feature catalog.}
\cite{chessformer-2ltBRzEHyd} reviewers explicitly demanded a
\emph{non-cherry-picked} catalog. Annotate the first 40 SAE features by
firing frequency; ship the remainder behind an anonymized URL with full
visualizations. Boring features count: ``square is occupied,''
``piece-on-d5,'' ``check.''

\textbf{[P0] Adversarial soundness check.}
Following \cite{BLOIB8CwBI-soundness}'s IMO-style protocol, adversarially
probe whether dynamic re-encoding prevents hallucinated state references in
LLAMIA's commentary (``the rook on c8 is attacking $\dots$'' when no rook on
c8). Compare to verbalized-tool baseline. Quantify factual board errors per
1000 commentary tokens.

\textbf{[P1] Human ecological validity of interventions.}
Show two annotated games to chess-experienced raters: one un-intervened,
one with the SAE-direction added. Ask raters to identify which followed an
instruction. \cite{Wxl0JMgDoU-maia2-sae} reviewer wbeH demanded this for
a Maia SAE; we should bake it in.

\textbf{[P1] Distinct from Karvonen et al.\ (Othello SAE) and Maia-2 SAE.}
Required differentiation paragraph: we operate on the
\emph{LLM-side projection} of an external policy, not the policy's own
latents. SAE features here are about how the LLM \emph{reads} the agent,
not what the agent \emph{computes}. Cited in
\cite{gBz9NMbvYS-maia2-sae,Wxl0JMgDoU-maia2-sae}.

\subsection{Scaling-law axes (insights that ``travel across the board'')}
\label{sec:todo_scaling}

Per call log, area chairs reward insights that generalize beyond the
specific paper. Multi-axis scaling is the cleanest such insight.

\textbf{[P0] LLM size $\times$ agent Elo (the headline 2D scaling figure).}
Train LLAMIA with LLM $\in\{$1B, 3B, 7B, 13B, 32B$\}$ (we have 7B/13B; add at
least 1B and 32B) crossed with agent $\in\{$Maia-1500, Lc0-T72 ($\sim$2200),
T80 ($\sim$2400), BT4 ($\sim$3200)$\}$. Plot for the four headline metrics
(BC, commentary, ICP, gameplay). Expected insight: minimum LLM size below
which agent quality stops mattering; minimum agent Elo below which
collaboration collapses to LLM-only. Frontier is diagonal.
Call log: ``so this is one access of computing -- how many units of compute
do you give birth date?'' -- here we operationalize that across both axes.

\textbf{[P0] Three-way GRPO comparison (Re-Tool style anchor figure).}
Identical reward, base model, rollout count for three runs:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Text-only GRPO:} no agent.
    \item \textbf{Tool-calling GRPO:} ReAct over Lc0 (exact \cite{toolrl2025}
    recipe).
    \item \textbf{Latent-internal GRPO (LLAMIA):} latent tokens +
    \texttt{<InvokeAgent>}.
\end{enumerate}
Reward and held-out win-rate vs.\ training step on the ICP task.
``How does the gap evolve?'' is the canonical retool-style figure.
Call log: ``how will multi-agent reinforcement learning with GRP work out?''
-- this is the answer.

\textbf{[P0] Avoid the term ``scaling laws.''}
\cite{DiffuSearch-A9y3LFX4ds} reviewers explicitly attacked ``scaling laws''
language without proper power-law fits. Use ``scaling trends'' or
``compute--performance curves'' unless we have $\geq 4$ decades and bootstrap
fits.

\textbf{[P1] Data-axis scaling.}
Stage-1 data $\in\{$1M, 5M, 25M, 50M$\}$ state--policy pairs;
Stage-2 data $\in\{$50k, 250k, 1M$\}$ instruction examples. Two scaling
curves per task. Compute the \emph{compute-equivalent improvement}:
``how many additional Stage-2 samples does internalization buy you over
text-only fine-tuning?'' Backs the ``$4\times$ data efficiency'' claim
already in the draft.

\textbf{[P1] Compute-allocation tradeoff (Pareto frontier).}
Fixed training-FLOPs budget; sweep allocation between LLM-side
parameters/data and agent-side search depth.
ICP and game-commentary should favor LLM-side; raw gameplay should favor
agent-side. Resolves the call-log point ``the search is not getting stirred
into the latent internalization, that is a problem.''

\textbf{[P1] Test-time compute Pareto.}
\#\texttt{<InvokeAgent>} calls per game vs.\ task performance. Distinguish
two budgets: LLM tokens (CoT length) and agent calls. Two-axis curve.
At fixed wall-clock, what is the optimal split? Generalizes the existing
$K$-token ablation.

\textbf{[P2] Reasoning-budget $\times$ task difficulty.}
\cite{kefUcn22mk-chessqa,LLM-CHESS-65R1Dbfwzk} demonstrated that reasoning
budget (low/med/high effort) interacts with task difficulty. Sweep CoT
length budget $\in\{0, 256, 1024, 4096\}$ tokens and report performance
per budget. Required for the verbalized-tool baseline to look honest.

\subsection{Failure-mode taxonomy and granular error analysis}
\label{sec:todo_failure}

\textbf{[P0] Verbalized-tool failure taxonomy.}
\cite{kefUcn22mk-chessqa,LLM-CHESS-65R1Dbfwzk} both built explicit failure
taxonomies that anchored their narratives. Build ours:
(i) tool-call format error,
(ii) hallucinated move string / nonexistent piece reference,
(iii) sound analysis but wrong final action,
(iv) ``no answer'' / refusal,
(v) plan abandonment / off-topic drift,
(vi) strategic blunder (the only ``honest'' error category).
For each system, report counts per category on a held-out 500-position set.
LLAMIA should eliminate (i)--(iv), preserve only (v) and (vi).

\textbf{[P0] Three-stage CoT-error decomposition.}
Apply \cite{yRxX01oRIi-cot-hurts}'s
$(\alpha_k^{\text{decomp}}, \alpha_k^{\text{solve}}, \alpha_k^{\text{summ}})$
decomposition to verbalized-tool baselines. Each verbalized step
contributes a noise term; latent re-encoding bypasses two of three.
Quantify per-stage contribution.

\textbf{[P1] LLAMIA failure-mode breakdown.}
When LLAMIA is wrong: (a) LLM-side reasoning, (b) agent-side, (c) interface
(latent token mis-conditioned). Confusion matrix across tasks.

\subsection{State annotation and commentary}
\label{sec:todo_commentary}

\textbf{[P0] Move-level vs.\ game-level decomposition.}
Same model on (a) move-level annotation and (b) game-level commentary;
plot the gap over baselines for each. Move-level = single-step (small
gap; tool call suffices); game-level = multi-step (large gap; re-encoding

\textbf{[P1] Annotation-accuracy and salience-targeting metrics.}
Define formally:
(i) \emph{annotation accuracy}: does commentary correctly identify
blunder/brilliancy/quiet for each move?
(ii) \emph{salience targeting}: does commentary emphasize moves the engine
flags as turning points (eval-second-derivative thresholds)?
Aggregate across $\geq 100$ games; binomial CIs.

\textbf{[P1] Counterfactual coverage.}
Subset of state-annotation set where ground-truth commentary discusses
counterfactual lines (``if Black had played \dots''). Score systems on
counterfactual coverage. Cleanest motivation for re-encoding: counterfactuals
require invoking the agent on a hypothetical state.

\textbf{[P1] Verbosity-binned comparison.}
\cite{toolrl2025}-style reviewers (and ZW8t Q5) ask whether LLAMIA wins by
being verbose. Bin systems by output-token count; show LLAMIA wins
\emph{within each bin}.

\textbf{[P2] Pedagogy diversity proxy.}
Diversity of strategic concepts mentioned per commentary as automatic proxy.
Cluster-count $\in \{$tactics, strategy, endgame, opening theory,
psychology$\}$.

\subsection{Puzzles: difficulty, interestingness, and rationale}
\label{sec:todo_puzzles}

\textbf{[P0] Counter-intuitiveness reward signal.}
Adopt \cite{TNZse5q2Tr-creative-puzzles}'s definition: shallow-search eval
$\neq$ deep-search eval. Operationalize via \texttt{lc0\_server} \texttt{/policy}
endpoint with $\text{nodes} \in \{1, 64, 1024\}$; report
$|\Delta \text{eval}|$ as the difficulty signal. Validate against Lichess
puzzle ratings (Pearson $r$).

\textbf{[P0] Human difficulty prediction with rationale.}
Beyond Spearman-$\rho$: have LLAMIA \emph{justify} its predicted difficulty
(``hard for $\sim$1600 because $\dots$''); raters score whether the rationale
is correct. \cite{TNZse5q2Tr-creative-puzzles} acknowledged the deepmind
generative-chess paper has a difficulty predictor without rationale; our
contribution is the explanation. $\kappa \geq 0.7$ across $\geq 3$ raters.

\textbf{[P1] Hard-puzzle subset (anti-saturation).}
\cite{kefUcn22mk-chessqa} added 100 Lichess puzzles rated 3000+ in
rebuttal -- GPT-5 high-effort solved only 26/100. Replicate: a
``LLAMIA-Bench-Hard'' subset at the top decile of Lichess difficulty.
Pre-empts ``benchmark saturates'' critique.

\textbf{[P1] Pre-cutoff vs.\ post-cutoff contamination test.}
Split puzzles by Lichess upload date $\leq$/$>$ each baseline LLM's
training-data cutoff. Run $z$/Fisher tests for accuracy difference.
Required by \cite{kefUcn22mk-chessqa}; chess web data is heavily scraped.

\textbf{[P2] Puzzle generation (stretch).}
Use LLAMIA to generate puzzles conditioned on a difficulty target. Have
humans rate authenticity. Appendix-only; cf.\
\cite{TNZse5q2Tr-creative-puzzles}.

\subsection{Ablations beyond the existing five}
\label{sec:todo_ablations}

Existing: Stage-1/2, InvokeAgent, $k$, agent-size, illegal-move analysis.
Add:

\textbf{[P0] Latent vs.\ symbolic-rich ablation.}
Replace $\vz_s$ with a textual representation of equivalent information
content (top-$k$ moves with full eval, WDL probabilities, principal
variations to depth 20, positional features). Train both at matched FLOPs.
The thesis collapses if symbolic-rich text closes the gap; this is the
cleanest possible ablation of \emph{representation form} given matched
\emph{information content}.

\textbf{[P0] $\vz_s$ vs.\ $\vh_s$ (raw vs.\ projected).}
Reviewer ZW8t's central question. Train LLAMIA with raw $\vh_s$
token-padded to $k\times e$ vs.\ projected $\vz_s$. If parity, drop the
projector claim.

\textbf{[P0] Random / zeroed / weak-engine latent ablations.}
\cite{DiffuSearch-A9y3LFX4ds}-style ``random world / oracle world / corrupted
world.'' Replace $\vz_s$ with: (a) Gaussian noise of matched shape and
norm, (b) zeros, (c) $\vz_{s'}$ from a corrupted FEN, (d) $\vz_s$ from a
weaker agent (Lc0-T72 instead of BT4). Shows information \emph{content}
matters, not just bandwidth.

\textbf{[P0] Future-paradigm ablation.}
\cite{DiffuSearch-A9y3LFX4ds} S-AA / S-ASA / S-AVAV / S-AVSAV. Train
LLAMIA where the latent encodes only:
(a) policy distribution, (b) value, (c) policy + value,
(d) policy + value + future-trajectory tokens. Show each ingredient adds.

\textbf{[P0] InvokeAgent placement policy.}
Three policies: every-move, learned-adaptive (current), random-matched-count.
Adaptive should beat both. Defends ``LLM learns \emph{when} to invoke.''

\textbf{[P0] Train/test data-leakage audit.}

\subsection{Paper-positioning insights from accepted vs.\ rejected papers}
\label{sec:todo_positioning}

Lessons from the digest of \texttt{paper\_reviews/}:

\textbf{Why accepted papers got accepted.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \emph{Convergent multi-method evidence}
    (\cite{Leela-lookahead-8zg9sO4ttV}): three independent techniques pointing
    at the same conclusion. We will mirror this for the SAE story.
    \item \emph{Pareto-improvement framing on a single dimension}
    (\cite{chessformer-2ltBRzEHyd}): same architecture, multiple wins.
    Apply: ``one model, one paradigm, multiple wins.''
    \item \emph{Counter-intuitive empirical finding tied to a clean
    theoretical model} (\cite{yRxX01oRIi-cot-hurts}). Our equivalent: dynamic
    re-encoding helps planning $64\%$ but only $1\%$ on single-step --
    explain via the $\alpha_k/\epsilon_k$ decomposition.
    \item \emph{Curated, deterministic benchmark}
    (\cite{kefUcn22mk-chessqa,LLM-CHESS-65R1Dbfwzk} both \emph{rejected}
    despite this -- it is necessary, not sufficient).
    \item \emph{Tournament-style head-to-head + binomial $p$-values}
    (\cite{afaakBqkvb-obscuro}).
    \item \emph{Live deployment / community engagement}
    (\cite{DiffuSearch-A9y3LFX4ds,Allie-bc2H72hGxB}: Lichess bot;
    \cite{TNZse5q2Tr-creative-puzzles}: GM testimonials).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Why rejected papers got rejected.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Marginal numerical gain not anchored to interpretable units
    (\cite{Maia4All-bwhLqFjsxd}: 51\%$\to$53\% reads cosmetic).
    Anchor to Elo-equivalent.
    \item Missing oracle / ceiling baselines (\cite{Maia4All-bwhLqFjsxd}).
    Add ``oracle agent call'' ceiling.
    \item Missing human baseline / human eval (\cite{kefUcn22mk-chessqa,
    gBz9NMbvYS-maia2-sae,Wxl0JMgDoU-maia2-sae}).
    \item Probing without causation
    (\cite{gBz9NMbvYS-maia2-sae,Wxl0JMgDoU-maia2-sae}). Always pair probes
    with interventions.
    \item Silent metric-swapping (\cite{lnIlH0hfek-mom} dropped Top-1/Top-2
    when results disappointed). \emph{Always} report all top-$k$.
    \item Missing limitations (\cite{lnIlH0hfek-mom}).
    \item Missing reasoning-model baseline
    (\cite{cmN8Wbvanr-card-games}).
    \item ``Descriptive'' benchmark with no mechanism story
    (\cite{LLM-CHESS-65R1Dbfwzk}). Each LLAMIA-Bench task must isolate a
    \emph{specific} verbalization failure mode.
    \item Imprecise terminology (\cite{DiffuSearch-A9y3LFX4ds}: ``scaling
    laws,'' ``compounding error''). Be specific.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Risks, traps, and contingency plans}
\label{sec:todo_risks}

\textbf{[P0] What if SAE features are not interpretable on the LLM-side
projection?}
Likely partial outcome -- the projector may disperse semantics. Mitigations:
(a) train SAEs on \emph{both} agent-side $\vh_s$ and LLM-side $\vz_s$;
report alignment; (b) frame the SAE section as
``existence proof of human-meaningful features'' rather than exhaustive labeling;
(c) lean on the causal-intervention experiment (which only requires \emph{some}
identifiable directions, not full coverage).

\textbf{[P0] What if LLM size $\times$ agent Elo scaling is flat in our range?}
Plan B: show the regime where it breaks (very weak LLM \emph{or} very weak
agent) and frame qualitatively.

\textbf{[P0] What if matched-fine-tune GPT-4o catches up?}
Likely on single-step. Plan B: lean harder on
sustained-interaction metrics, where re-encoding makes a structural difference.

\textbf{[P0] What if the radar charts show LLAMIA losing on public benchmarks?}
The honest framing is ``parity on public, win on low-data.''
Pre-write this narrative now so the paper does not over-claim.

\textbf{[P1] What if InvokeAgent's measured impact is prompt-sensitive?}
Likely. Robustness check: rerun the InvokeAgent ablation under three
different ICP prompt templates; report variance. Also test top-$p$ sampling
in addition to greedy (\cite{BLOIB8CwBI-soundness}-style robustness).

\textbf{[P1] What if entropy collapses during GRPO?}
\cite{TNZse5q2Tr-creative-puzzles,lnIlH0hfek-mom} both flag this for chess
RL. Apply diversity-filtering (\cite{TNZse5q2Tr-creative-puzzles} \S 2.1);
cite Wang 2025, Cui 2025, Yu 2025 (DAPO).

\textbf{[P1] What if Lc0 self-play games leak into LLAMIA-Bench?}
Audit and report (\S\ref{sec:todo_ablations} ``Train/test data-leakage'').

\textbf{[P2] What if behavior-cloning gains read cosmetic?}
Anchor every \% delta to an Elo-equivalent number, mirroring
\cite{CvjXlsBLCX-strength-est}'s round-robin Elo conversion.
