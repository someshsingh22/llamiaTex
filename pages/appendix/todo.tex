\subsection{Critique of the current draft and the call log}
\label{sec:todo_critique}
% =============================================================================

The 2026-04-27 call log is a planning conversation, not a specification. It
contains correct intuitions but several framings that do not survive
scrutiny. Recording the disagreements explicitly so we do not silently
inherit them.

\paragraph{Reject: the ``low-data regime'' framing as the LLAMIA-Bench
organizing principle.}
The call log proposes ``in-the-wild vs.\ in-distribution'' / ``low-data
regime'' as the axis that defines LLAMIA-Bench versus public benchmarks.
This conflates four distinct things and is empirically wrong on several of
its examples.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Puzzle interestingness and human difficulty are not
    low-data.} Lichess hosts $\sim$3M tactical puzzles with crowd-derived
    difficulty ratings (Glicko-style), aggregated solver attempt rates,
    success curves per Elo bucket, and theme labels.
    \cite{TNZse5q2Tr-creative-puzzles} explicitly used these ratings as a
    crowd-validated signal. Calling the difficulty/interestingness task
    ``low-data'' is incorrect.
    \item \textbf{Time-format and Elo-gap behavior cloning are not
    low-data.} Lichess publishes monthly database dumps with billions of
    rated bullet/blitz games and explicit time-control tags. The cap on
    these splits is task definition (what does ``$\pm$500 Elo gap'' mean
    operationally?), not data scarcity.
    \item \textbf{GM25 \emph{is} genuinely low-data} (a few hundred to a
    few thousand games per identified GM, depending on the player), as is
    \textbf{game-level commentary} (Agadmator-class transcripts number in
    the low thousands, properly aligned even fewer).
\end{itemize}
Conflating these into one axis means the bench reads as a grab-bag and
invites reviewer LEvg/HUaR-style ``what is this benchmark actually
testing?'' criticism. We need a functional axis instead.

\paragraph{Reject: framing LLAMIA-Bench as a benchmark of difficulty.}
The point of LLAMIA-Bench is not that the tasks are hard. It is that they
\emph{discriminate} between systems with different access to an agent:
text-only, verbalized-tool, and latent-internalized. A hard task that does
not discriminate (e.g.\ Lichess Elo-3000 puzzles, where every system that
can solve them can solve them) is useless for our story. A medium task that
\emph{does} discriminate (e.g.\ instruction-conditioned planning under a
stated weakness) is essential.

\paragraph{Reject: the ``two radars'' presentation.}
Two radars looks defensive (``here are our wins'') and forces an artificial
public-vs-bench split that does not exist for tasks like commentary, where
the public and LLAMIA-Bench versions are the same task at different
horizons. \textbf{One radar with proper spoke organization is cleaner}
(\S\ref{sec:todo_one_radar}).

\paragraph{Reject: the existing baselines.}
ChessGPT-7B/13B, GPT-4o, and LLaMA-3 405B are 2024-vintage. By
NeurIPS-2026 review, the meaningful frontier comparators are:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Frontier reasoning models}: Claude Opus 4.7,
    GPT-5/o4-mini-high, Gemini 2.5 Pro, DeepSeek-R1/V3.1,
    Qwen3-235B-A22B-thinking. Reviewers will reject as outdated any paper
    whose strongest baseline is GPT-4o.
    \item \textbf{Open-weight reasoning models}: Llama-4 Maverick,
    Qwen3-32B-thinking, DeepSeek-R1.
    \item \textbf{Tool-RL baselines}: Re-Tool, ToolRL \cite{toolrl2025},
    Search-R1, AgentR1 -- the right comparison for ``RL-optimized
    verbalized tool use,'' which is the strawman our thesis attacks.
    \item \textbf{Latent-CoT baselines}: Coconut (Hao et al.), and any
    2025--2026 implicit-reasoning paper -- the right comparison for
    ``passing continuous information through the LLM.''
    \item \textbf{Domain specialists}: Maia-2 (BC), Allie
    \cite{Allie-bc2H72hGxB} (BC + skill calibration),
    ChessFormer / GAB \cite{chessformer-2ltBRzEHyd} (gameplay + per-Elo
    fidelity), Lc0-BT4 / Stockfish 17 (gameplay ceiling),
    \cite{TNZse5q2Tr-creative-puzzles} (puzzle generation).
\end{itemize}
The current ChessGPT-IFT row should be \emph{removed} from the main table,
not refreshed. ``IFT chess LLM'' is no longer the natural baseline; the
natural baseline is a \emph{matched-recipe} text-only or tool-using
RL-trained model with the same backbone.

\paragraph{Reject: the SFT framing of the methodology.}
Section~\ref{sec:methodology} (current draft) describes Stage-1 alignment
+ Stage-2 SFT. By the time we ship, we need GRPO/RL-trained results, with
SFT as the warm-start. Re-frame the methodology to be SALT(SFT) +
SALT(RL); SFT-only becomes one row in the ablation, not the main story.

\paragraph{Accept (with caveats): the SAE story is correct in spirit.}
The call log's $\pi$ vs.\ $\pi'$ SAE experiment is the right idea and we
should keep it. Caveat: as
\cite{gBz9NMbvYS-maia2-sae,Wxl0JMgDoU-maia2-sae} prove, feature
visualization alone gets a paper rejected. The experiment must be paired
with causal interventions, baselines (linear probes, activation patching),
and human ecological validity.

\paragraph{Accept: the four-task taxonomy from the call log.}
``Behavior cloning, behavior stylometry, state annotation, commentary,
puzzles, ICP'' is the right \emph{set} of tasks. The mistake is grouping
them by data regime; we group them by \emph{collaboration competency}
(\S\ref{sec:todo_repositioning}).

\subsection{Repositioning: what LLAMIA-Bench actually measures}
\label{sec:todo_repositioning}
% =============================================================================

\paragraph{Cohesive thesis-aligned definition.}
\textbf{LLAMIA-Bench is a benchmark of \emph{LLM--agent collaboration
competence}.} A task belongs in LLAMIA-Bench if and only if it satisfies
\emph{all three} of the following inclusion criteria:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Heterogeneous information.} The task cannot be solved
    well by language alone or by an agent's symbolic outputs alone; it
    requires composing continuous policy/value information with natural
    language understanding.
    \item \textbf{Discrimination across access modes.} The task is
    designed (and verified) to produce different scores for systems with
    text-only access, verbalized-tool access, and latent-internalized
    access to the same agent. A task on which all three score equally is
    a public benchmark, not a LLAMIA-Bench task.
    \item \textbf{Sustained interaction in scope.} The task naturally
    extends to multi-turn or multi-move use of the agent (even if a
    single-turn version is scored) so that dynamic re-encoding can be
    studied.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Four collaboration competencies as the spoke groups.}
Each LLAMIA-Bench task attaches to exactly one of four competencies. This
replaces the call log's GDU/GDM partition with a finer, audit-friendly
taxonomy.

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{C1 -- Policy Fidelity under Instruction (PFI).}
    Can a system \emph{modulate} a policy given language and
    \emph{recognize} a policy from rolled-out games?
    Tasks: cross-Elo behavior cloning (\textsc{BC-Elo}); time-format
    cloning (\textsc{BC-Time}); GM-individual cloning (\textsc{BC-GM});
    Maia-equivalence (\textsc{BC-Maia}); behavior stylometry
    (\textsc{Stylo-Elo}, \textsc{Stylo-Time}, \textsc{Stylo-ID}).
    \item \textbf{C2 -- State Comprehension with Justification (SCJ).}
    Can a system explain \emph{why} a state is hard, interesting, equal,
    or won, to a target audience?
    Tasks: human-difficulty prediction with rationale
    (\textsc{Diff-Rat}); puzzle interestingness with rationale
    (\textsc{Int-Rat}); position evaluation with reasoning
    (\textsc{Eval-Rat}); blunder vs.\ brilliancy classification with
    rationale (\textsc{Move-Class}).
    \item \textbf{C3 -- Strategic Adaptation from Language (SAL).}
    Can a system steer planning trajectories given verbal cues?
    Tasks: instruction-conditioned planning win-rate against a calibrated
    opponent (\textsc{ICP-Win}); opening-prep adaptation
    (\textsc{ICP-Opening}); time-pressure adaptation (\textsc{ICP-Time});
    instructed material-trade (\textsc{ICP-Trade}).
    \item \textbf{C4 -- Sustained Narrative Generation (SNG).}
    Can a system maintain a coherent narrative over evolving state,
    integrating policy and value over many moves?
    Tasks: game-level commentary (\textsc{Comm-Game}); post-mortem
    generation (\textsc{Comm-Post}); planning narrative
    (\textsc{Comm-Plan}, the language counterpart of \textsc{ICP-Win});
    move-level annotation as a \emph{baseline} for the sustained version
    (\textsc{Comm-Move}).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Where data regime fits.}
Data regime is an \emph{orthogonal} experimental variable, not a benchmark
organizer. Each competency has both well-resourced and resource-scarce
subtasks. We control for it explicitly via a \texttt{data-regime} column in
the bench manifest (one of \texttt{abundant}, \texttt{moderate},
\texttt{scarce}) and report scaling behavior per regime where it informs
the story.

\paragraph{Where ``public'' fits.}
``Public benchmark'' is also orthogonal -- it is an indicator of
\emph{external comparability}, not a thesis claim. We tag tasks that have
existing standardized eval (\textsc{BC-Maia} on Maia-1900 split,
\textsc{Eval-Rat} on Lichess puzzle solve, gameplay Elo on standard
opponents) as \texttt{external-compare}, and tasks introduced by us as
\texttt{novel-eval}, in the same manifest. Both ride on the same radar.

\paragraph{What gets removed from the bench.}
Apply the discrimination criterion mercilessly. The current Behavior
Cloning row of Table~\ref{tab:chess_benchmarks} reports a single MAIA
accuracy; this is a task on which a tool-call to Maia-1900 already
saturates (Maia output \emph{is} the answer). We move it from the radar
to a single anchoring number elsewhere. Similarly, raw gameplay Elo on
standard opponents stays, but only as a single row -- not a spoke.

\paragraph{What gets added.}
The taxonomy demands tasks that we have not yet defined cleanly:
\textsc{Comm-Plan} (the language counterpart of ICP, where the system
narrates its plan against a stated weakness),
\textsc{ICP-Time} and \textsc{ICP-Opening} (broader adaptation than
endgame-only),
and \textsc{Stylo-Time} / \textsc{Stylo-ID} (currently only
\textsc{Stylo-Elo} is in scope). Definitions go in
\texttt{appendix/dataset.tex} (currently empty).

\subsection{Modern (2026) baseline grid}
\label{sec:todo_baselines}
% =============================================================================

\textbf{[P0] Replace the entire baseline section.}
Group baselines by what they isolate. Every system receives an explicit
\emph{access-mode} tag and a \emph{recipe} tag.

\paragraph{Group A -- Frontier general LLMs (text-only).}
Claude Opus 4.7, GPT-5 / o4-mini-high (we will run whichever is publicly
available at submission time), Gemini 2.5 Pro,
DeepSeek-R1, Qwen3-235B-A22B-thinking.
Access-mode \texttt{text}; recipe \texttt{frontier-zero-shot}.
Purpose: anchors the ``what does pure language understanding give you?''
floor. \emph{Not the strawman}: their use is a control, not the headline
comparison.

\paragraph{Group B -- Frontier general LLMs with verbalized tools.}
Same Group A models with ReAct-style access to:
(i) Lc0-BT4 \texttt{/policy} endpoint (top-$k$ moves with $P, V, N, Q$),
(ii) Lc0-BT4 \texttt{/analyze} (search results to depth 20--30),
(iii) Stockfish 17 \texttt{/analyze} (alternative engine, defends against
``it's just Lc0''). Access-mode \texttt{verbalized-tool}; recipe
\texttt{frontier-react}. Purpose: the standard tool-calling baseline.
Detailed protocol pseudocode mandatory (\S\ref{sec:todo_rigor}).

\paragraph{Group C -- RL-trained tool-using LLMs (the real strawman).}
This is the comparison that defends the thesis -- tool calling \emph{with}
RL-optimized invocation. We need to actually run this:
(i) Re-Tool / ToolRL recipe applied to our chess tools, on the same
backbone as LLAMIA (Qwen3-13B / Vicuna-13B);
(ii) Search-R1 with chess as the search domain;
(iii) AgentR1.
Same Stage-2 data, same RL recipe (GRPO or DAPO),
matched compute. Access-mode \texttt{verbalized-tool-rl}; recipe
\texttt{matched-rl}. \textbf{If LLAMIA does not beat this group, the paper
has no thesis.}

\paragraph{Group D -- Latent-CoT / continuous-reasoning baselines.}
Coconut (Hao et al.), latent multi-agent collab \cite{latentmas2025},
implicit-reasoning baselines from 2025--2026. Adapted to use Lc0 as the
``other agent'' where the architecture supports it; treated as
\texttt{text}-only otherwise. Purpose: defends the claim that simply
having continuous communication is not enough -- it must be
\emph{cross-architecture}, our heterogeneous-agents argument.

\paragraph{Group E -- Domain specialists (per-task ceilings).}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textsc{BC-Maia}: Maia-2 (multi-Elo single model).
    \item \textsc{BC-Time, BC-Elo}: Allie \cite{Allie-bc2H72hGxB}.
    \item \textsc{BC-GM}: best-of-cohort
    \cite{lnIlH0hfek-mom}-style per-GM model.
    \item Gameplay Elo: Lc0-BT4 (search), Stockfish 17, ChessFormer
    \cite{chessformer-2ltBRzEHyd}.
    \item \textsc{Diff-Rat, Int-Rat}: deepmind generative-chess predictor
    + (no rationale baseline; gap is the contribution).
    \item \textsc{Comm-Move}: SCC \cite{zang2019automated}.
    \item \textsc{Comm-Game}: \emph{none} -- we are introducing the task.
\end{itemize}
Purpose: per-spoke ceilings on the radar. Drawn as separate dots.

\paragraph{Group F -- Matched-recipe LLAMIA ablations.}
Same backbone, same Stage-2 data, same RL recipe; what changes is the
projection:
\texttt{LLAMIA-text} (no projection),
\texttt{LLAMIA-tool} (same backbone with verbalized tools, RL-trained),
\texttt{LLAMIA-static} (latent projection, no \texttt{<InvokeAgent>}),
\texttt{LLAMIA-full} (the full recipe).
Purpose: clean within-recipe comparison. This is the row that defends the
thesis better than any external comparison.

\paragraph{Group G -- Heterogeneous-encoder cross-check.}
Re-train LLAMIA replacing Lc0-BT4 with Maia-2's encoder
(genuine human-aligned policy) and with Stockfish-NNUE evaluator
(non-transformer). Defends ``the paradigm is not Lc0-specific.''

\paragraph{Decoding-protocol matrix.}
Every baseline must be reported under \emph{both} (i) greedy / strict
no-retry (cf.\ \cite{lnIlH0hfek-mom}) and (ii) self-consistency (5 samples,
majority/best). Single number conceals method effects; both numbers expose
them.

\paragraph{Minimum compute parity.}
Each frontier baseline gets the same per-task token budget as LLAMIA
(including LLAMIA's latent tokens counted as text-equivalent in the
``effective context'' column). No more ``LLAMIA wins because we let it
think for 8k tokens vs.\ 1k for the baseline.''

\subsection{Methodology: SALT(SFT) + SALT(RL), not SALT alone}
\label{sec:todo_method_reframe}
% =============================================================================

\textbf{[P0] Re-frame the methodology section as two-phase, modern-RL.}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Phase 1 -- SALT(SFT).} Stage 1 (projector alignment) +
    Stage 2 (interleaved instruction tuning). This is the warm-start; it
    is no longer the headline result.
    \item \textbf{Phase 2 -- SALT(RL).} GRPO (or DAPO) over rollouts that
    include latent tokens and \texttt{<InvokeAgent>} calls. Reward
    decomposed as: outcome reward (game result, BC accuracy, ICP
    win-rate, judge-score on commentary), format reward (legal moves,
    valid invocations), and a \emph{collaboration-shape reward}
    encouraging the model to invoke at decision-relevant moments.
    Diversity-filtering against entropy collapse (cite Wang 2025,
    Cui 2025, Yu 2025 / DAPO; protocol from
    \cite{TNZse5q2Tr-creative-puzzles}).
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{[P0] Backbone refresh.}
Vicuna-13B is 2023-vintage. Move to Qwen3-13B-Instruct or Llama-4 8B/70B
as the modern backbone (which one is decided by inference economics +
\texttt{<InvokeAgent>} compatibility). Vicuna-13B becomes a single legacy
row in the ablation, not the headline.

\textbf{[P0] Three matched RL runs as the headline collaboration figure.}
Re-Tool-style figure: reward and ICP-win-rate vs.\ training step for
\texttt{LLAMIA-text}, \texttt{LLAMIA-tool}, \texttt{LLAMIA-full} (Group F)
on identical reward, base, and rollout count. Replaces the current
\texttt{kt}/\texttt{comm}/\texttt{icp} subfigure trio as the core
methodology figure.

\subsection{Per-competency experiment plan}
\label{sec:todo_per_competency}
% =============================================================================

\paragraph{C1 / PFI experiments.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{[P0] Maia-equivalence at policy level.} For
    $X \in \{1100, 1300, 1500, 1700, 1900\}$, compare LLAMIA-instructed-as-X,
    Maia-X, depth-matched Lc0. Metrics: top-1 move match, KL to actual
    human-Elo-X moves, calibration error on the
    \cite{Allie-bc2H72hGxB} skill-gap definition (mean
    $|\text{achieved}-\text{target}|$ Elo).
    \item \textbf{[P0] Round-robin Elo} across LLAMIA-instructed-as-X
    cells, Maia-X cells, depth-matched Lc0; pairwise win-rates →
    iterative Elo (\cite{CvjXlsBLCX-strength-est}).
    250 games/cell, bootstrap CIs.
    \item \textbf{[P0] Cross-Elo extrapolation.} Train on Elo bins
    $\{1300, 1700\}$; test on $\{1100, 1500, 1900\}$. Verbalized tools
    cannot interpolate the agent's policy; latent projection should.
    \item \textbf{[P0] Stylometry top-1, top-2, top-3, with held-out
    real GMs.} Mandatory after \cite{lnIlH0hfek-mom} reject. Diagonal
    dominance, split-half consistency, full confusion matrix.
    Bradley--Terry baseline from \cite{CvjXlsBLCX-strength-est}.
    \item \textbf{[P1] Lichess deployment.} $\geq 1000$ games per
    instructed-Elo target; per-game post-survey;
    IRB exemption \textbf{45 CFR 46.104(d)(2)(ii)}.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{C2 / SCJ experiments.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{[P0] Counter-intuitiveness signal.} Operationalize
    difficulty via shallow-vs-deep Lc0 disagreement on
    \texttt{nodes} $\in \{1, 64, 1024\}$
    (\cite{TNZse5q2Tr-creative-puzzles}); validate against Lichess
    puzzle ratings (Pearson $r \geq 0.6$).
    \item \textbf{[P0] Difficulty + rationale joint scoring.} Two scores
    per item: difficulty Spearman-$\rho$, rationale correctness
    (rater-judged, $\kappa \geq 0.7$, three raters).
    Headline: rationale correctness gap is the contribution; pure
    Spearman is shared with deepmind generative-chess predictor.
    \item \textbf{[P0] Hard-puzzle subset (anti-saturation).}
    Top-decile Lichess puzzles ($\sim$3000+) plus an evergreen ``unseen
    after $T$'' subset for contamination control.
    \item \textbf{[P1] Pre-cutoff vs.\ post-cutoff contamination test.}
    Per-baseline cutoff date; $z$/Fisher tests on the gap.
    \item \textbf{[P1] Audience conditioning.} Same item, two prompts:
    ``explain to a 1200'' vs.\ ``explain to a 2200.'' Score readability
    + correctness. Tests whether C2 systems can audience-target.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{C3 / SAL experiments.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{[P0] \textsc{ICP-Win}: turn-by-turn win-rate curves.}
    Per move: win-rate (Monte Carlo via Lc0, $\geq 100$ rollouts), KL to
    base policy, theme-match (LLM-judge classifier, validated against
    $\geq 100$ human labels at $\kappa \geq 0.7$).
    Plot expected win-rate vs.\ move number across systems.
    \item \textbf{[P0] Theme broadening.} Beyond ``weak in endgames,''
    add \textsc{ICP-Opening} (``weak in Sicilian Najdorf''),
    \textsc{ICP-Time} (``opponent in time pressure''), \textsc{ICP-Trade}
    (``trade pieces to a winning endgame''),
    \textsc{ICP-Complicate} (``complicate the position''). Per-theme
    win-rate; per-theme follow-through analysis.
    \item \textbf{[P0] Causal steering via SAE direction
    (replicates the ICP behavior).} See \S\ref{sec:todo_sae}.
    \item \textbf{[P1] Adversarial instructions.} Instruct toward
    a \emph{wrong} theme (``opponent weak in openings'' when they're
    weak in endgames); does the system blindly follow language or
    integrate the agent's evaluation? A latent-conditioned model
    should sometimes refuse; a tool-caller will follow blindly.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{C4 / SNG experiments.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{[P0] Move-level vs.\ game-level decomposition.} Same
    model on both versions of the task; report the gap. Move-level
    closes; game-level does not (verbalization compounds).
    \item \textbf{[P0] Replay-graph metrics.} Per game, plot:
    engine-eval trajectory, commentary attention to played move,
    annotation accuracy. Define \emph{narrative continuity} =
    correlation between commentary salience and engine-eval second
    derivative (turning points). \emph{Narrative grounding} =
    fraction of commentary spans referencing positions consistent with
    the actual board. Report both.
    \item \textbf{[P0] Counterfactual coverage.} Subset of gold
    commentary discussing counterfactual lines; score systems on
    counterfactual-line coverage. Cleanest motivation for re-encoding.
    \item \textbf{[P0] Verbosity-binned comparison} (ZW8t Q5).
    \item \textbf{[P1] Functional-understanding study.} Already in
    \S\ref{sec:human_study}; extend to an ICP-rationality task and a
    stylometry blind-tasting task. Tiered raters
    ($\sim$1400 / 1700 / 2000 / $\geq$2200).
    \item \textbf{[P1] LLM-judge validation.} GPT-4o-as-judge against
    blind human majority on 100 cases ($\kappa = 1.0$ target);
    \cite{yRxX01oRIi-cot-hurts}-style.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Mechanistic interpretability via Sparse Autoencoders}
\label{sec:todo_sae}
% =============================================================================

The call log nominated this as a flagship analysis.
\cite{gBz9NMbvYS-maia2-sae,Wxl0JMgDoU-maia2-sae} (both \emph{rejected})
prove that visualization alone is insufficient; reviewers demand causal
interventions, alternative-method comparisons, and human ecological
validity. The plan below incorporates those constraints from the start.

\textbf{[P0] Convergent multi-method evidence.}
Following \cite{Leela-lookahead-8zg9sO4ttV}'s template, every claim about
the $K{=}16$ projected tokens supported by three lines of evidence:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \emph{Sparse autoencoder} (top-$k$ or JumpReLU; dictionary
    $\in\{8\text{k},32\text{k}\}$, $L_0 \approx 32$, ghost-grad).
    Hyperparameters jointly optimized for reconstruction and
    Concept-Coverage AUC against a curated chess-concept set
    (\cite{gBz9NMbvYS-maia2-sae}-style).
    \item \emph{Linear probes} for each concept on $\vz_s$ and
    $\vh_s$. \cite{BLOIB8CwBI-soundness}: probes can be decodable but
    causally inert -- always pair with intervention.
    \item \emph{Activation patching} between (clean FEN, corrupted FEN)
    pairs where Lc0's top move flips but a weak engine's does not. Direct
    \cite{Leela-lookahead-8zg9sO4ttV} adaptation.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{[P0] $\pi$ vs.\ $\pi'$ feature-delta experiment.}
For one fixed position $s$, harvest $\vz_s^{\text{base}}$,
$\vz_s^{\text{ICP}}$, $\vz_s^{\text{Maia-X}}$.
Decompose via SAE; show sparse delta $\Delta f$.
Hypothesis: the Maia-X delta in our latent space matches the
\emph{actual} Maia-X policy delta from base Lc0. Headline:
\emph{instruction reproduces what fine-tuning does to a separate model}.

\textbf{[P0] Causal steering.}
Identify the SAE direction most predictive of an ICP instruction.
Ablate the language instruction at the prompt; \emph{add} the SAE
direction at inference. Recovery of the win-rate delta is causal evidence.
Missing in \cite{gBz9NMbvYS-maia2-sae,Wxl0JMgDoU-maia2-sae}; one of the
reasons they were rejected.

\textbf{[P0] Future-square probes (implicit lookahead).}
Linear probes on $\vz_s$ predict principal-variation squares 1, 3, 5
moves into the future. \cite{Leela-lookahead-8zg9sO4ttV} \S 3.3
adaptation. Mate-in-$N$ vs.\ quiet position split; gap argues for
inherited implicit search.

\textbf{[P0] Per-token attribution.}
Ablate each of the $K{=}16$ tokens individually; plot log-odds reduction
across tasks. Group tokens by ablation effect (gameplay-critical /
commentary-critical / ICP-critical).

\textbf{[P0] Adversarial soundness.}
\cite{BLOIB8CwBI-soundness}-style IMO oracle test: does dynamic
re-encoding eliminate hallucinated piece references in commentary? Per
1000 commentary tokens, count factual-board errors.

\textbf{[P0] Key figure: 1 position $\times$ 3 instructions $\times$ 3 views.}
3 rows (base / ICP / BC) $\times$ 3 columns (board with arrows,
top-$K$ activated SAE features, board heatmap of attribution). One ICP
example, one BC example.

\textbf{[P1] First-$N$ non-cherry-picked feature catalog.}
\cite{chessformer-2ltBRzEHyd}-style: annotate first 40 features by firing
frequency; remainder behind anonymized URL.

\textbf{[P1] Human ecological-validity check.}
Two annotated games shown to chess-experienced raters; identify which
followed an instruction. \cite{Wxl0JMgDoU-maia2-sae} reviewer wbeH demand.

\textbf{[P1] Distinct-from-Karvonen paragraph.}
Required differentiation: we operate on the LLM-side projection of an
external policy, not the policy's own latents. Features here capture how
the LLM \emph{reads} the agent.

\subsection{Risks, traps, and contingency plans}
\label{sec:todo_risks}
% =============================================================================

\textbf{[P0] LLAMIA loses to Group C (matched RL tool-callers) on
single-step tasks.} Likely. Plan: lean entirely on sustained-interaction
metrics, where re-encoding makes a structural difference. Pre-write the
``parity on single-step, win on sustained'' narrative now.

\textbf{[P0] LLAMIA loses to Group A (frontier reasoning models) at any
spoke.} Possible (e.g.\ Claude Opus 4.7 may already write strong
commentary). Plan: report the loss honestly; show that the win is
structural (Group F, matched recipe) rather than absolute.

\textbf{[P0] SAE features on the LLM-side projection are not
interpretable.} Mitigations: dual SAE (agent-side $\vh_s$ and LLM-side
$\vz_s$), report alignment; lean on causal interventions (which only
need \emph{some} identifiable directions); frame as existence proof.

\textbf{[P0] LLM-size $\times$ agent-strength scaling is flat in our
range.} Plan: show the regime where it breaks (very weak LLM \emph{or}
agent) and frame qualitatively.

\textbf{[P1] InvokeAgent impact is prompt-sensitive.} Robustness check:
three ICP prompt templates; report variance.

\textbf{[P1] GRPO entropy collapse.} Diversity filtering
(\cite{TNZse5q2Tr-creative-puzzles}); cite Wang/Cui/Yu 2025 (DAPO).

\textbf{[P1] Lc0 self-play games leak into LLAMIA-Bench.}
Audit table (\S\ref{sec:todo_ablations}).

\textbf{[P2] BC gains read cosmetic.} Anchor every \% delta to an
Elo-equivalent (\cite{CvjXlsBLCX-strength-est}).
